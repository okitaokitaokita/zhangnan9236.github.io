Smart City Surveillance Video Analysis System

Intelligent monitoring system to analyze urban surveillance videos — detecting abnormal events and providing traffic statistics to improve city safety and efficiency.

Problem

Urban surveillance systems generate massive amounts of video data that are difficult to monitor manually in real-time. City safety departments need an automated intelligent monitoring system that can detect abnormal events (e.g., sudden crowd running, traffic congestion, unauthorized area intrusion) and provide quantitative traffic statistics.

Data

VIRAT public dataset — a large-scale outdoor surveillance video benchmark containing multiple scenes (parking lots, intersections, public areas) with frame-level annotations for 12+ activity types including pedestrian walking, vehicle turning, and group interactions.

Approach

Designed a multi-stage video analytics pipeline: (1) Adaptive background subtraction using GMM; (2) Optical flow algorithms (TV-L1, CLG) for dense motion estimation; (3) YOLOv5 for real-time object detection and tracking; (4) Rule-based event detection logic combining motion magnitude, object density, and trajectory analysis; (5) Visualization dashboard with hourly traffic flow curves and group size distribution histograms.

Outcome / Impact

Achieved mAP@0.5 > 0.85; reduced false positive rate by 30% through multi-model fusion; system processes video at ~15 FPS on GPU.

My Contribution

Led video preprocessing and background modeling; implemented GMM background subtraction; developed optical flow motion analysis; created traffic statistics visualizations; conducted pipeline validation.

Dec 2022 – Jun 2023 Individual (BSc Thesis)

Stock Price Prediction using LSTM

Deep learning model to predict future stock prices from historical data, addressing non-linear temporal relationships in financial time series to support data-driven investment decisions.

Problem

Financial markets exhibit complex non-linear temporal dynamics that traditional statistical models (e.g., ARIMA) struggle to capture. This thesis investigated whether LSTM networks can outperform traditional time series models for stock price prediction.

Data

Historical daily stock price data from Yahoo Finance API, covering 5+ years (2017–2022) for Chinese A-share stocks. Features: open, high, low, close, volume (~1,200+ records per stock) plus engineered indicators (MA5, MA20, RSI, MACD).

Approach

End-to-end pipeline: (1) Data collection and cleaning (yfinance, pandas); (2) Feature engineering (moving averages, RSI, MACD); (3) Min-Max normalization + 60-day sliding window; (4) Multi-layer LSTM architecture (2 LSTM layers + dropout + dense) in PyTorch; (5) Grid search hyperparameter tuning; (6) Evaluation with RMSE, MAE, directional accuracy; (7) Comparison against ARIMA and linear regression baselines.

Outcome / Impact

RMSE 40% lower than ARIMA, 55% lower than linear regression; directional accuracy 62% (vs 50% random baseline); stable results via 5-fold time-series cross-validation.

My Contribution

Independently completed the entire pipeline — literature review, data collection, feature engineering, model design, training, evaluation, visualization, and thesis writing.
